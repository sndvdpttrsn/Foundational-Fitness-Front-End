Dynamic Warm Up

You've heard that stretching is important. However, static stretching should be
saved for the end of a workout, not the beginning. When warming up, it is important
to move the parts of the body that you will be exercising. Follow the following dynamic
stretches in order to warm up your body before exerting yourself for your respective exercise.
A typical dynamic warmup lasts about fifteen minutes.

Pushups

Pushups are a very basic and effective exercise to workout your arms.
Note: The typical amount of reps for a set of pushups is between 5 and 30.
Note: The typical amount of sets for a pushup exercise is between two and four.
Note: The typical amount of rest time for this exercise between sets is between a minute and/or 90 seconds.

  1. Start in a plank position, arms extended fully and you should be on all fours.
  2. Lower your chest to the ground, bending your arms.
  3. After holding for half a second to one second, use your arms to lift your chest off the ground.
  4. Return to the plank position.
  5. Repeat steps two through four for the duration of the absolute value of your respective reps in a set.

Situps

Situps are a very basic and effective exercise to work out your abdomen.
Note: The typical amount of reps for a set of situps is between 5 and 30.
Note: The typical amount of sets for a situp exercise is between two and four.
Note: The typical amount of rest time for this exercise between sets is between a 30 seconds and/or 90 seconds.

  1. Start by laying on the ground, back to the ground and stomach facing upward.
  2. Bend your legs such that your feet are flat on the ground and your knees are facing upward.
    • Note: Your legs should be at a 45 degree angle.
  3. Lift your upper body up using your abdomen and touch your chest to your knees.
  4. Hold your chest to your knees for one to three seconds.
    • Note: The amount of time depends on fatigue, strength level and endurance level.
  5. Lower your upper body back to the ground, back on the ground, stomach to the sky.
  6. Repeat steps three through five for the duration of the absolute value of your respective reps in a set.

Squats

Squats are a very basic and effective exercise to work out your upper legs.
Note: The typical amount of reps for a set of squats is between 5 and 30.
Note: The typical amount of sets for a squats exercise is between two and four.
Note: The typical amount of rest time for this exercise between sets is between a sixty seconds and/or 90 seconds.

  1. Start in standing position.
  2. Raise your hands and touch your ears.
    • Make sure your hands stay at your ears for the duration of the exercise.
  3. Lower your legs to approximately 90 degrees.
  4. Hold for one to three seconds.
    • Note: The amount of time depends on fatigue, strength level and endurance level.
  5. Raise your legs back to standing position.
  6. Repeat steps three through five for the duration of the absolute value of your respective reps in a set.

Calf Raises

calf Raises are a very basic and effective exercise to work out your lower legs.
Note: The typical amount of reps for a set of calf raises is between 5 and 30.
Note: The typical amount of sets for a calf raises exercise is between two and four.
Note: The typical amount of rest time for this exercise between sets is between a sixty seconds and/or 90 seconds.

  1. Start in standing position.
  2. Raise your hands and touch your ears.
    • Make sure your hands stay at your ears for the duration of the exercise.
  3. Raise your heels to approximately forty five degrees.
  4. Hold for one to three seconds.
    • Note: The amount of time depends on fatigue, strength level and endurance level.
  5. Lower your heels back to standing position.
  6. Repeat steps three through five for the duration of the absolute value of your respective reps in a set.
